# Orion Spacecraft Hull Specifications (Draft Version 1.2)

The Orion spacecraft hull is heavily engineered with a multi-layered proprietary carbon-composite shell designed specifically to withstand deep-space radiation and micrometeoroid impacts. 

## Key Materials
1. **DuraWeave Titanium Mesh**: The outermost layer providing structural kinetic energy deflection.
2. **Aerogel Insulation Sheets**: Used predominantly in the mid-layer for extreme temperature regulation, keeping the interior cabin at a stable 72 degrees Fahrenheit even in direct solar radiation or lunar eclipse darkness.
3. **Lead-Polished Inner Shell**: Ensures zero radiation bleeds into the sensitive crew compartments.

## Structural Capabilities
The core framework is capable of supporting over 14 Gs of sustained acceleration during emergency launch abort scenarios. It utilizes a honeycombed interlocking pattern to reduce overall mass without sacrificing tensile strength.

## Planned Updates
The upcoming revision (v2.0) will replace the Aerogel Insulation with a newly developed "Cryo-Silk" layer, which is expected to reduce the vehicle mass by approximately 800 kilograms.
